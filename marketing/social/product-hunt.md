## (b) Maker's first comment (~150 words)

Hey Product Hunt 👋

I built BraidFlow because hair braiders are running a real business inside their Instagram DMs — twenty messages to pin down one appointment, and then a no-show torches a 6-hour slot that can't be refilled.

The generic schedulers don't help: Square, Acuity and Calendly are built around 30-minute meetings and treat a deposit as a paid add-on. For a braider, the deposit is the whole point.

So the deposit is the default here. A client picks a style, picks a slot, and pays through Stripe before the chair is held. Balance is paid in person, and 100% of the deposit stays with the braider — no cut. The whole week runs from one dashboard.

It's a live demo with sample data — no signup, poke at everything. I built it solo and I'd genuinely like to know: braiders and clients, what would make you actually use this? Tear it apart.

— Mohammed (@elkamohammad1988)

## (c) Short description variants (for the listing)

**Variant 1 (product-forward):**
BraidFlow is a booking-and-deposit page built for hair braiders. Clients pick a style, pick a slot, and pay a deposit through Stripe before the chair is held — balance paid in person. Braiders run the whole week (calendar, clients, revenue, availability) from one dashboard, and keep 100% of every deposit.

**Variant 2 (problem-forward):**
Braiders book through DMs and eat the cost of no-shows. BraidFlow replaces the back-and-forth with a shareable booking page that takes the deposit up front, locks the slot, and puts the calendar, clients and revenue in one place. Built for 4–8 hour appointments, not 30-minute meetings.

**Variant 3 (short + punchy):**
A booking page for hair braiders that gets you paid before the appointment. Deposit-first via Stripe, guest checkout, day-blocks for real life, and one dashboard for the week. No cut of your service price.

**Variant 4 (maker/tech-forward):**
Deposit-first booking for hair braiders, built solo with Next.js 14 and Stripe. Webhook-driven booking state machine, timezone-correct scheduling, guest checkout, and a zero-config demo you can deploy without a backend. Live and pokeable.
